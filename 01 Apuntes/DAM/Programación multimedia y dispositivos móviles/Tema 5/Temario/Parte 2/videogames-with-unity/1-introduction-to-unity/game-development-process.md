# Game Development Process

Source: https://ecm-pmdm-unity.gitbook.io/videogames-with-unity/1.-introduction-to-unity/game-development-process

Building a game involves a series of strategic **steps**, from conceptualization to the final production phase:

**Step 1: Conceptualization**

The initial stage revolves around defining the **game's** **core** **idea**. It includes outlining the **storyline**, **setting**, **characters**, and **key** gameplay **mechanics**. This phase serves as the blueprint for the entire project.

**Step 2: Designing the Game Scenes**

After establishing the game's concept, the next step involves detailed **planning** and **designing** of the various **scenes**. Each scene should seamlessly contribute to the overall narrative and gameplay experience.

**Step 3: Development**

With the design in place, the development phase focuses on bringing the game scenes to life. This encompasses **coding**, **creating** art **assets**, and **integrating** **sound** **effects** and **music**. A modular approach is recommended to facilitate future expansions.

**Step 4: Testing**

Rigorous testing is crucial to **ensure** the **game's** **stability** and **playability**. This involves identifying and rectifying bugs, and making necessary adjustments to gameplay based on feedback.

**Step 5: Launch and Expansion**

Once the game meets all quality standards, it is **ready** **for** **launch**. Post-launch involves **expanding** the **game**, adding new content, and continually enhancing the player experience.
